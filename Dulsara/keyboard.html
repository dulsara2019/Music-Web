Keyboard Instruments


Keyboard instruments are any instruments played by depressing levers, buttons,
or keys to produce sound.
The most common of these instruments are the piano and organ, but there’s a wide variety
keyboard instruments in existence.
Nearly all keyboard instruments, especially those associated with Western music, have keys corresponding to
notes in the chromatic scale and run from bass at the left side of the keyboard to treble at the right.
As one of the most versatile musical instrument families, keyboards have amassed great importance and popularity.
The keyboard allows a performer to play several notes at once and in close succession to one another,
a feat that few other instruments can accomplish.
Because nearly any composition can be played on a keyboard, whether it’s chordal harmonies, a single melody or a
combination of the two, the keyboard has been utilized by nearly every major composer since the 16th century.

Did You Know?

The pianoforte, more commonly known as simply just the piano, is one of the world’s most popular instruments.
Piano has contributed greatly to modern and classical music
It was first created in early 1700s.
It has originated from a harpsichord and since has taken on different forms
of grand piano, upright piano, digital pianos and finally keyboards and synthesizers as well as hybrid pianos.
The piano has a keyboard (a row of keys) that is struck by the pianist's fingers and thumbs.
The keys cause the hammers to strike the strings and create sound.
Pianos can withstand around 20 tons of tension on an ongoing basis.
The piano is known as "The King of Instruments".
The average medium sized piano has around 230 strings.
The piano is the most commonly played instrument by musicians in the world.
A modern piano has around 7500 parts.
Pianos are affected by humidity, temperature, location changes and direct sunlight exposure.
The piano is capable of the widest range of tones
The piano is often used when composing and writing music.
It is used in a variety of music styles including classical,
pop, jazz, rock, blues, folk, country, and many more.
